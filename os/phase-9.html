Post-Failure Diagnosis Flow
Trigger: task card with status === REJECTED
4th button "⊛" appears on marketing task card alongside ✓/↺/▶. Lazy-fetches rejection comments from ClickUp on click via GET /api/tasks/:id/rejection-comments. Opens RoundTableModal with pre-loaded task context (name, platform, rejection count, full rejection feedback).
Session runs with Harper/Wren/Sage/Morgan pre-seated
Post-failure default roster. Persona context injected with full task context (brief, draft history, rejection comments). Morgan's finalize prompt outputs resetTarget: STRATEGIST | DRAFTER | NONE in YAML frontmatter.
STRATEGIST reset
Strategy brief was wrong/vague. Subtasks 2–4 → BACKLOG. Strategy subtask → OPEN. Rejection count cleared on parent. Pipeline restarts from top.
DRAFTER reset
Brief was clear; draft failed execution. Draft subtask → OPEN. Critic + polish → BACKLOG. Prior rejection comments preserved as drafter context on next run.
NONE — Critic miscalibration
No subtask reset. Morgan's diagnosis written as [ROUND TABLE NOTE — CRITIC CALIBRATION] ClickUp comment. Sage picks it up via existing comment-scraping on next critic run. "⊛ Post Critic calibration note" button in panel fires the write.

AI Citation Integration — Meltwater Research (July 2026)
LinkedIn is the #2 most-cited source in AI-generated answers (behind YouTube). Individual profiles generate ~75% of LinkedIn AI citations vs. ~25% from Company Pages. Structure drives citability — bullets, named entities, and quantitative data outside pure prose. This research was embedded as standing knowledge flowing through all relevant pipeline layers.
1
Harper (Strategist) — citation priority signal in strategy brief
On LinkedIn posts where AI discoverability is a strategic objective, Harper flags citationPriority: yes in the brief. This tells Wren to weight citation-readiness in drafting. Not required on every post — Harper's call.
2
Wren (Drafter) — citation-readiness requirements baked into DRAFTER-DOCTRINE.md
LinkedIn posts must include: (a) at least one structured element (bullet list, numbered list, or bolded subheading), (b) at least one named entity or quantitative data point outside pure prose, (c) a deliberate personal-vs-page channel assignment (individual profiles cited ~3× more). Wren drafts to these standards.
3
Sage (Critic) — Q6 citation-readiness added to content-critic.js (LinkedIn only)
New Q6 checks all three citation conditions. Q6 failure is never an automatic rejection — surfaces as APPROVED WITH FLAGS → FLAGS FOR POLISH. Q1–Q5 determine the verdict.
4
Devon (SEO Agent) — Meltwater findings as core doctrine; citation-readiness scoring in Round Table
Devon evaluates structural citability (0–4 score), channel fit (personal vs. page), and query alignment. Devon does not evaluate voice or brand fit — that's Harper/Wren/Sage/Jordan. Live data feed deferred until Elizabeth's SEO research is complete.
5
Jordan (CMO) — LinkedIn citation dynamics as standing channel principle in CMO-DOCTRINE.md
Jordan weighs citation dynamics as a platform distribution reality — not a reason to override voice or brand fit, but one variable in the platform mix. Devon scores citation-readiness specifically in Round Table sessions.
