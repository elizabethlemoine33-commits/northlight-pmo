AI Citation Integration — Meltwater Research (July 2026)
LinkedIn is the #2 most-cited source in AI-generated answers (behind YouTube). Individual profiles generate ~75% of LinkedIn AI citations vs. ~25% from Company Pages. Structure drives citability — bullets, named entities, and quantitative data outside pure prose. This research was embedded as standing knowledge flowing through all relevant pipeline layers.
1
Harper (Strategist) — citation priority signal in strategy brief
On LinkedIn posts where AI discoverability is a strategic objective, Harper flags citationPriority: yes in the brief. This tells Wren to weight citation-readiness in drafting. Not required on every post — Harper's call.
2
Wren (Drafter) — citation-readiness requirements baked into DRAFTER-DOCTRINE.md
LinkedIn posts must include: (a) at least one structured element (bullet list, numbered list, or bolded subheading), (b) at least one named entity or quantitative data point outside pure prose, (c) a deliberate personal-vs-page channel assignment (individual profiles cited ~3× more). Wren drafts to these standards; they apply to LinkedIn Personal and LinkedIn Page only.
3
Sage (Critic) — Q6 citation-readiness added to content-critic.js (LinkedIn only)
New Q6 checks all three citation conditions. Q6 failure is never an automatic rejection — surfaces as APPROVED WITH FLAGS → FLAGS FOR POLISH. Q1–Q5 determine the verdict. Code change: lib/skills/content-critic.js uses spread operator to conditionally include Q6 block when platform.toLowerCase().includes('linkedin').
4
Devon (SEO Agent) — Meltwater findings as core doctrine; citation-readiness scoring in Round Table
Devon evaluates structural citability (0–4 score), channel fit (personal vs. page), and query alignment. Devon does not evaluate voice or brand fit — that's Harper/Wren/Sage/Jordan. Live data feed (competitor keywords, GEO score trends) deferred until Elizabeth's SEO research is complete.
5
Jordan (CMO) — LinkedIn citation dynamics as standing channel principle in CMO-DOCTRINE.md
Jordan weighs citation dynamics as a platform distribution reality — not a reason to override voice or brand fit, but one variable in the platform mix. Jordan does not run the citation analysis alone; Devon scores it specifically.
Post-Failure Diagnosis Flow
Trigger: rejectionCount > 0 on a task card
A 4th button "⊛ Round Table" appears on the marketing task card. Opens RoundTableModal with Harper/Wren/Sage/Morgan pre-seated and full task context loaded (brief, draft, rejection history).
Session runs in post-failure mode (isPostFailure: true)
Persona context includes task context (rejection comments). Morgan's finalize prompt adds resetTarget field to OKF YAML frontmatter.
STRATEGIST reset
Strategy brief was wrong/vague. Subtasks 2–4 → BACKLOG. Strategy subtask → OPEN. Comment written to parent. Rejection count cleared.
DRAFTER reset
Brief was clear; draft failed execution. Draft subtask → OPEN. Critic + polish → BACKLOG. Rejection comments preserved as drafter context.
NONE — Critic miscalibration
No subtask reset. Morgan's diagnosis written as [ROUND TABLE NOTE — CRITIC CALIBRATION] ClickUp comment. Sage picks it up via existing comment-scraping on next critic run.
